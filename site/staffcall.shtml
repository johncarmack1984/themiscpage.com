back to staff

Over the past two weeks, I, John Carmack, webmaster of TMP, have been working feverishly to get the site back up in time for the estimated relaunch date of July 25th. With much work still undone, I have taken a short break from my HTML tweaking to send you this message.
We here at The Miscellaneous Page (and, yes, it really is we now) enjoyed the casual environment that having a really ugly site brought. However, since people got headaches everytime they viewed pages with purple backgrounds and green text, we thought it was time for a change. While we'll be much more attractive, we will maintain our reputation of being a ridiculous site.
That said, I would like to get to the real point of this message. After all of this HTML tweaking over several cups of caffeinated beverages, I've decided to take on a staff. Positions will begin work on August 10th. Reason being, I leave Subway on August 10th, and that will be my first opportunity to give someone a quick runthrough of how to update their section of the site. For those intersted and able, the jobs and requirements are below. To apply, fill out the mail form at the bottom and we'll get in touch with you. And it is okay to apply for more than one job, ifyou like.

All Editors and Managers must know HTML.

Awards Manager
The awards manager will manage The Impressive "Hey, you've got a pretty nifty little page there" Award section and who it is given to. For this, you must be kind enough to forgive petty things, but harsh enough to be able to let people know when their site really isn't good enough for an award. There's lots of grey area there, and this is probably one of the easier jobs. It is preferrable but not required if you go through me before adding the award to the site.

Editorials Manager
The Editorials manager will be required to write one editorial every week. Topic isn't important, just so long as the editorials keep coming. This will prevent the problem we had with TMP going stale for a month straight without submissions. This person will also receive and edit articles sent by visitors and post them on the page.

Downloads Manager
This guy's (or girl's) job will be to seek out weird stuff on the internet such as movies, programs, sounds, and other such things that don't really have a better place to stay than good ol' TMP. This one isn't too hard if you're on the internet as much as I am, and I know there's several out there who are.

FanTale Editor
Call them FanTales, FanFics, or just Stories, they're all just entertaining stuff. This person will recieve and edit stories sent by visitors and post them on the page, in addition to writing some on their own if they like.

Moderators
The only job that does not require knowledge of HTML. This is a popular one, and I've only got use for three. One slot is already filled. You have to meet strict requirements to be one, and very few folks do.

Jokes Manager
While this is my personal favorite section, I would be willing to relinquish it to someone who could manage competently. Whoever takes this job will probably want to sign up for a few mailing lists that send jokes, although it is not a requirement, it would give you plenty of fodder and you could update quite frequently, if you wanted to.

Music Editor
This person would seek out MIDI and MP3 files on the internet and put them in the music section. You would also recieve music files from visitors and post them on the page. This person must have an ear for music and not put up bad sounding stuff. For instance, if I send you one of my midis, you trash it immediately. ;-)

Pics & Art Editor
This person needs to be a graphic artist, or something resembling it. I'm actively seeking someone for this spot, but if you feel that you have a shot, please apply. This person will need to make image for the staff. Style is not important, just as long as we've got something cool looking to go next to our names so we can feel really good about ourselves. You would also manage your section as the other editors and managers on the staff.

Surveys Manager
This person will need to come up with a new survey question every one or two weeks and post results for each survey. Please note that I am in the process of removing the BeSeen quizlet and installing a javascript poll program. A good sense of humor is important for this job, as to keep the surveys intersting. As a general rule, if you ask this question: "Which came first, the chicken, or the egg?" You will be fired and hit with a cheese log. Apart from that, easy job.

Webring Manager
Ah, yes. This job takes webring experience, and not too many people have it. Anyone who does, I'd love to hear from you. This webring is pretty much routine maintenance for me, and I'd like someone to help out. Heck, I'd let you run the dang thing entirely if you want.

As I said, all of these positions will be effective on August 10th, 2000. If you have any questions, simply send me an email. Have a nice day, everyone.
