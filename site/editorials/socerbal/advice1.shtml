A Little Bit of Advice...
With all the new Zelda games coming out, let me give you some advice. No matter what you do, DO NOT use a strategy guide unless ABSOLUTELY necessary! It's not worth it. I know from experience.

I got Zelda on Christmas day of 1998. Later that day, I bought a strategy guide. When I got home, I started reading it. It was an official strategy guide, so it had a foreword from Mr. Miyamoto, and a summary of all the Zelda games. After I read those, I couldn't stop. I started reading about what to do in Kokiri Forest. Then I started playing the game. I used the guide for EVERYTHING. At the time, I was happy I had it, but now I realize that all it did was lessen the incredibility of my gaming experience.

It took me less than two weeks to beat the game. I thought it was so cool that I beat it so quickly, but it wasn't. I now realize that if I was smart, I would have struggled with the game for hours. It would have made it so much more fun if I wouldn't have known exactly where to go in Jabu-Jabu's belly or what to do in the Water Temple. I wish I could have had to work to find the carpenters when they were stuck in Gerudo Fortress instead of pausing the game to read which passage to go through, which door to enter. But most of all, I wish I hadn't read the locations to the skulltullas and heart pieces, where to sell the masks at, or what to do to get Biggoron's sword.

I am not saying strategy guides are bad, I'm just saying that you should only use them when you have been stuck in the same place for a week, or have fought the same boss ten times a day for ten days getting beat each time. Definitely don't use them for the mini games, it only takes away the pleasure you get from beating them. If you can't figure something out after five hours, do something else, find a heart piece, talk to somebody, then go try again.

Basically what I'm trying to say is, don't ruin the game. Use your brain instead of a guide! When Zelda:Gaiden comes out, do not use a strategy guide to beat it. It will make the game a lot more fun. I guarantee it.
