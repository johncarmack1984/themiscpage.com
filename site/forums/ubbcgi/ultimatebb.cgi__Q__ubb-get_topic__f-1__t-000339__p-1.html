We may have evolved, but we also need the nutrients from meat to survive. If eating meat is so wrong, then why is it an essential part of our diet? Literally, without meat, which we were built to eat, we would not be able to live healthily.

Now, I'm sure you're going to say that we (as in Americans) have access to vitamins to supply us with those nutrients, and you're right about that. But what about in other countries? Are they wrong for eating meat in order to live healthy lives?

However, I don't see how this line of thinking has anything to do with defending the viewpoint that says torturing, killing, and then eating other living creatures is a morally acceptable thing to do. Just because we're CAPABLE of doing something doesn't nessecarily make it moral.


As for the torture aspect of this, I'm pretty sure it's illegal to torture animals.
I could be wrong about this, but for the greater part of their lives, cattle live in relative comfort. Lots of food and no real dangers (except hoof and mouth).

And with regards to the killing part, how is that bad? It is done in a quick manner, no slow or torturous pain for the animals. And then it's done. Seems like a pretty humane way to die, when compared to being eaten alive by a wolf.

Also, what about hunting? The animal is in no way tortured, and is usually killed instantly. The animal is then (normally) used for food of some sort. Is this wrong? What about the fact that we have a HUGE overpopulation of deer right now? Should we not hunt them, just let them roam free to be a nuisance? And then there's the torture part of it. If you do not do something about the overpopulation, the deer start to become sick, and then die a slow and painful death. This is what nature is like, but that's not immoral. So what's wrong with keeping the population down, and keeping the deer from suffering, by hunting, killing, and then making use of the body by eating it?
