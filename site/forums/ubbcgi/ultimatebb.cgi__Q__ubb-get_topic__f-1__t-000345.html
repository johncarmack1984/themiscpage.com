Where was I?

No offense taken. Just to clairify something... I don't believe in magic anymore. Out-of-body experiences are nothing but lucid dreams; tarrot cards and astrology are superstitions that can be disproven; humans do possess are very limited amount of "telikenetic" abilities--I've actually done them once or twice myself, but haven't had any more success for a while--but that can be explained in other ways than the supernatural.

Other things, like remote viewing, seem to me like they might be possible. I did considerably well when I tested myslef for this ability. It's too bad you can't put this to much use, other than a little entertainment. Being able to guess eight cards correctly out of twenty isn't much use in everyday life.

I haven't attempted lucid dreaming for a while, but I had some interesting results back when I was still trying. Before I started trying to have a lucid dream, I had about five dreams a year. Yet I had that many dreams in one week back when I was really trying to become "lucid".

I don't know why I just gave up like that. Maybe I got bored after a few weeks. Whatever the case, I think it's time I started trying again...

But back to magic. It's no wonder I believed in magic so strongly... it came from my strong belief in the Bible. If you believe in the Bible, you have to believe that magic exists also. At first, I just switched my belief that magic was caused by demons to it being caused by natural forces. It just took me a while to realize that it didn't exist.

My parents think I'm crazy for not believing magic, tarrot cards, etc. is real, by the way. My parents drilling a belief in magic into my head, and the Bible, were my only reasons for believing it existed. In other words, I'll believe it when I see it.
