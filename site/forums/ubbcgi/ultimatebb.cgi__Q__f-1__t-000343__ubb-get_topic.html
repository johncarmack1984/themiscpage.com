An interesting dream story.

Some time ago, I was staying at a friend's house. This particular friend likes to listen to classical music to lull himself to sleep. Well, we were drifting off into the land of slumber, and I was near that time of blissful rest between the rift of conciousness and the subconcious. Midway through this journey, Wagner's "In the Hall of the Mountain King" came on my friend's classical radio station. As the notes and rhythms melded themselves into my quickly developing dream, I found myself standing before a looming Parthenon-type structure on top of a mountain somewhere in the Himalayas. I looked behind myself to see only white-capped mountain tops and sharp cliffs. In front of me, steps leading into the massive temple beckoned to me, all the while, the melodies of the song playing along in my mind. I walked up the stairs into the lavish white marble interior of the building. Once inside, I saw a large banquet table filled with all of the foods one person could desire. It was like a Thanksgiving feast one should imagine a family of thirty would dine upon. At the head of this table sat a dark haired, rotund, bearded fellow wearing kingly robes and a crown that tilted slightly to the left, giving him rather a cocky appearance. The fellow spoke.

"Welcome! Welcome! Please, help me to eat these delightful dishes, for surely not one man alone can consume such a great deal!"

Reluctantly, I sat down in a chair that had not been behind me until only just then. As I reached for a particularly delicious-looking portion of goose (for my so-called journey had apparently quite piqued my hunger) I noticed that the king was now standing and had begun to walk towards me, with a jolly look on his somewhat chubby face. For some reason, this startled me and I stood and began to back up (once I stood up, the chair disappeared). He looked confused, and asked me why I was behaving so oddly. To this I had no response, and I continued to move backwards through grand chamber. I made the mistake, however, of backing into one of the magnificent pillars that filled the chamber. This caused a great tremor throughout the structure, and a few small bits of marble came falling from the ceiling.

As the song reached its intense musical climax, the chamber began to collapse. I ran, dodging debris and falling marble columns. With each strike of the bass drum, a new obstacle would come across my path. Just as I reached the exit, and as the song came to its close, I leapt out the exit as the last of the building came crashing down upon itself. My landing coincided with the final run of the song's unusual melody. With the final strike of the orchestra, the collapse of the structure was done, and I was pulled away from the fallen structure by my subconcious. My two minute, thirty second dream ended with a final view of the mountain king, standing amidst the rubble, shaking his fist in the air as if to find some way to exact revenge on the one who had destroyed his temple.

I awoke the next morning with a profound sense of guilt. But then I drank some orange juice, and now I'm okay.
