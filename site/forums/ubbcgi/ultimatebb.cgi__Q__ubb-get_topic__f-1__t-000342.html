quote:
Congress shall make no law respecting an establishment of religion, or prohibiting the free exercise thereof; or abridging the freedom of speech, or of the press; or the right of the people peaceably to assemble, and to petition the government for a redress of grievances.

This, my friends, is the first amendment that protects just about anything you could possibly do. This is the first amendment to the Constitution. Theoretically, this means I can say whatever I want, do whatever I want, and so on--as long as it's peaceful. However, there are many restrictions on this law.

Take this board as example. If I were to openly post a message, along the lines of "Bob, go **** yourself..." or something similar, this would not be appropriate and my actions would not be protected.

Why you ask? First of all, these can be considered slander is not protected at all. Second, fighting words that tend to incite immediate violence, offensive speech to a hostile, potentially violent audience, false statements likely to cause panic, disorder and safety hazards aren't protected. Finally, Carmack owns this board--therefore it is personal property. He can make almost any rule he wants and get away with it.

Now, how does this apply to the situation? Manson, if you can name a situation where something could not offend anybody, I'd love to read it. This shirt was aimed towards non-atheists as a whole, but focused in on those who believe in Jesus--it had the word "believe" on it with "lie" in bold. This is almost the equivalent of me going up to you and saying what you believe in is wrong. And finally, the school is owned by the county government. Because of this, they can restrict what you can do, wear, etc.
