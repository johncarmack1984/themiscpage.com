The following is meant to be read with all seriousness, and treated with respect. Thank you.

On April 19th, 1995, at 9:02 AM in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma, no more than 63 miles away from my house, the Alfred P. Murrah building was blown up from underneath by a Ryder truck containing a bomb. In the following chaos, many rescue attempts were made, and many succeeded, but many failed also. After days of uncertainty, it was determined that 168 people had been killed in the blast and many more injured. Windows all over downtown Oklahoma City had been blown out, and many people grieved the loss of friends or family. Today marked the five year anniversary of this tragic event in Oklahoman, and American history. Please remember the people in the Oklahoma City bombing in your prayers, and never forget that this, the worst terrorist attack ever made on American soil, our darkest hour, was turned into a glimmer of hope by the people of Oklahoma and is now used as a witness to others than we can stand through tragedy. Remember Oklahoma City and the 168 lives given on April 19th, 1995 at 9:02 AM.

Thank you for reading.
